## Incident Report
### Summary
On 2026-05-02, our organization was targeted by a ransomware attack, resulting in multiple hosts being encrypted. The incident response team quickly activated to mitigate the damage and restore operations as soon as possible.

## Root Cause
The most likely attack path is a phishing campaign that led to a failed login attempt, followed by a successful login using an administrator account. The attackers then used this access to spread the ransomware across multiple hosts.

## Runbook Compliance
The incident response team followed the runbook's guidelines, but there are a few areas where compliance could be improved:

*   **Initial Triage**: Step 2.1 (Activate the IR team and switch to out-of-band comms) was executed correctly.
*   However, the team did not fully follow step 2.3 (Capture ransom notes, file extensions, and affected hostnames). This information is crucial for forensics and attribution.

## Recommended Next Actions
To mitigate the attack's effects:

1.  **Restore from backups**: Use the most recent verified clean backup to restore critical systems.
2.  **Implement security patches**: Apply any necessary security patches to prevent similar attacks in the future.
3.  **Rebuild compromised hosts**: Rebuild affected hosts from known-good images to prevent re-infection.
4.  **Notify relevant parties**: Inform law enforcement, regulatory bodies, and other stakeholders about the incident.

**Next steps:**

1.  Conduct a thorough post-incident review to identify areas for improvement in the response plan and execute necessary updates to the runbook.
2.  Provide training to staff on user responsibilities and reporting procedures to prevent similar incidents.
3.  Review and update the security patches and configurations to prevent future attacks.

This incident highlights the importance of having a comprehensive incident response plan, regular security audits, and employee education programs in place to mitigate the impact of cyber threats.
